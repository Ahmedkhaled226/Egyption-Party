Amr Diab

"Amr Abdel Basset Abdel Aziz Diab (born October 11, 1961), known by his artistic name Amr Diab, and his nickname is El Hadaba, is an Egyptian singer, composer, and actor. He is considered one of the most famous singers in the Arab world, as his albums achieved huge sales, and many of his songs were translated into several languages, the most important of which are: English, Russian, Croatian, Bulgarian, Turkish, Albanian, Hindi, Greek, and French. . Amr Diab has been distinguished by introducing new instruments into his music and creating different types of songs throughout his history."

Hamaki

"Mohamed Ibrahim Mohamed Hamaki (born November 4, 1975), known by his artistic name Mohamed Hamaki, is an Egyptian singer, and one of the singers on the Arab singing scene. He is the first Egyptian artist to represent a member of the jury of The Voice: The Sweetest Voice in its Arabic version in the fourth and fifth seasons, which was shown on MBC channels. He is also a member of the jury for The Voice Kids: The Sweetest Voice in its third season, which was also shown on MBC channels."

Tamer Ashour

"Tamer Ashour (born January 2, 1984) is an Egyptian singer, composer, and songwriter. He was born in Damietta. He released more than 15 songs from 2004 to 2008, and among the most famous of these songs are: “Thank you,” “We parted,” “Everyday,” “Mabqash,” “I want to tell you,” “You chose,” and “ In the nights of my torment with you,” and other songs that were the main reason for Tamer’s fame, and made him one of the most important emerging singers at that time, and one of the stars who would become a big name in the world of singing in the future."

Hamza Namira
